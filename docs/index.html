About me

I am responsible at Microchip Technology Inc for the Rapid Control Prototyping tool for dsPIC and PIC32 microcontrollers.

Embedded-system and applied-math enthousiast, I realized many projects on signal processing and control theory. In such project, 90% of the effort (my estimate) is taken by low level programming tasks and hardware design. Time for simulation is reduced and projects often ends-up with a simple PID algorithm tuned by successive trials.

In 2004, I designed a blockset to deploy Simulink models on dsPIC Micro-Controllers Unit (MCU). The model base design saves the low-level programming task and allows to concentrate on control algorithms.

From 2005 to 2009, I applied this rapid prototyping scheme to complete my PhD thesis on Biologically inspired robotics (fly’s visual system from 2005-2009) where it reduced the time to convert a Simulink/dSPACE algorithm to our OSCAR II autonomous robot.

In 2010, I joined MOVEA, a spin-up from CEA-Leti where I designed and implemented Data fusion algorithm for Inertial Measurement Unit for sport applications. The use of model base design reduced the Time to Market: from experimental simulated algorithms to final products.

For scientists, company, or students’ projects, the model-based design allows focusing on high level algorithm where added value is.

These pages present customs projects and information’s related to Rapid Control Prototyping tools. All are based on the Microchip blockset (provided for free) and a board equipped with a dsPIC or PIC32.

Few articles and patents are listed on Google Scholar.
